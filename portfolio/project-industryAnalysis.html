Analysis of IT Salary Survey in Germany (2018-2020)

Statistical Computing in Practice Project (SAS software)


Goal: to make decision regarding getting an IT job in Europe
A 22 pages statistical report revealing some interesting facts.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive analysis of the dataset, covering 14 key factors including age, salary, gender, city, and more
  • Visualizations including box plots, bar charts, scatter plots, and correlation analyses to effectively communicate insights and trends within the dataset
  • Identification of key technologies and programming languages in demand within the German IT industry
  • Statistical tests and analyses to address specific objectives

Technologies Used

Two-Sample t-test
Correlation Analysis
ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)

Insights and Discussion

  • The dataset "IT Salary Survey for EU region (2018-2020)" may not fully represent the entire European Union, but it provides valuable insights for the German IT industry.
  • Immigration decisions are influenced by multiple factors, including cultural differences, quality of life, cost of living, and political climate.
  • Significant employment and salary gaps exist between male and female IT professionals, highlighting the need for addressing gender disparities in the industry.
  • No conclusive evidence was found to suggest a significant decrease in mean annual salaries during the pandemic.
  • Germany emerges as an attractive destination for IT professionals considering emigration, offering higher annual salaries compared to other sectors.
  • Proficiency in English, Python, and Java is advantageous for IT professionals in Germany.
  • Working for a product company is recommended, as it aligns with the higher salary trends observed in the dataset.
  • Further research and analysis can explore specific industry trends and the long-term effects of the pandemic on IT sector employment and compensation dynamics.
